Tokyo

About the Destination

Tokyo, Japan's busy capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The city is a masterpiece of efficiency and culture, offering world-class dining, unique fashion in Harajuku, and the serene beauty of the Imperial Palace gardens.

The city is globally famous for its incredible food scene and technological innovation. From the world's busiest intersection at Shibuya Crossing to the majestic Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, Tokyo offers a constant contrast between the future and the past. Visitors can explore the electronic town of Akihabara or enjoy the cherry blossoms at Ueno Park.

Historical Significance

Originally a small fishing village named Edo, Tokyo became the imperial capital in 1868. Despite being destroyed twice in the 20th century, the city rose from the ashes to become one of the most powerful and fascinating cities in the world, deeply rooted in Samurai heritage while leading the world in modern design.

Fast Facts

  • Language: Japanese
  • Currency: Japanese Yen (¥)
  • Transport: Subway, Bullet Train (Shinkansen)

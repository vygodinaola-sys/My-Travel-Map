Rome

About the Destination

Rome, the "Eternal City," is the capital of Italy and a global treasure of history, art, and architecture. With nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture on display, it is an open-air museum where ancient ruins like the Forum and the Colosseum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire.

The city is globally famous for its breathtaking landmarks and incredible culinary heritage. Within its borders lies the Vatican City, headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church, home to St. Peter’s Basilica and the Michelangelo-frescoed Sistine Chapel. Visitors can toss a coin into the Trevi Fountain or climb the famous Spanish Steps for a true Italian experience.

Historical Significance

According to legend, Rome was founded in 753 BC by Romulus and Remus. It grew to become the center of the Roman Republic and then the Roman Empire, shaping the foundations of Western law, art, and government. Today, Rome is a bustling modern metropolis that manages to preserve its layers of history in every stone and piazza.

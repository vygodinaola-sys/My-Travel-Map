New York City

About the Destination

New York City, often called "The Big Apple," is a sprawling metropolis at the mouth of the Hudson River. As a global hub for finance, fashion, and entertainment, it consists of five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Its iconic skyline is defined by skyscrapers that tell a story of ambition and modern history.

The city is globally famous for its energy and landmarks. From the bright neon lights of Times Square to the historic Statue of Liberty, NYC offers a constant stream of discovery. Visitors can find world-class theater on Broadway, explore massive green spaces like Central Park, or visit world-renowned museums like the Met and MoMA.

Historical Significance

Originally settled as a Dutch trading post called New Amsterdam, the city became a beacon of hope for millions of immigrants arriving through Ellis Island. Today, NYC is a vibrant tapestry of cultures, reflected in the diverse neighborhoods of Harlem, Chinatown, and Little Italy, each contributing to the city's unique spirit.

Fast Facts

  • Transport: Subway, Yellow Cabs
